🎟️ Book these ahead

Anything with a timed entry sells out — book the official sites early. Everything else you can buy on the day. (You book; this page only links out.)

Priority: the Eiffel summit and the Versailles palace slot go first (they sell out, and the palace is closed Mondays) → then the Louvre and Orsay timed slots → Notre-Dame is free but take a timed slot in its official app (opens 2–3 days ahead). Sainte-Chapelle + Conciergerie and the Panthéon are buy-at-the-door. The RER C train to Versailles needs no reservation — buy at the machine (~€4 one-way, Zone 4).

🟢
Outdoors forgives everything. Gardens, viewpoints, Montmartre, the Seine, market streets — no tickets, no schedule. Missed something? Slot it into any gap or the last morning.
🟠
Timed slots are the only hard constraint. The Louvre, Orsay and Versailles need a booked time. Sainte-Chapelle, Conciergerie, Notre-Dame and the Panthéon are buy-at-the-door. Miss a slot and it's gone — just rebook on the official site.
🍽️
Food is flexible. If a bistro is full or you're not hungry, pick another option or grab one from the backups below. Book the one or two dinners that really matter; leave the rest open.
🧭
Swap like for like. Days are built by neighbourhood, so move a missed Left-Bank stop to another Left-Bank day rather than crossing the city. One anchor per half-day, the rest floats.
🌧️
Rain? Switch to an indoor anchor (a museum, the Opéra, a covered passage, the Marais) and push the outdoor plan to the next dry half-day.
🗓️
Closing days — the thing people miss. The Louvre & Orangerie close Tuesdays; Orsay closes Mondays; Versailles closes Mondays. Map these six days onto your dates around the closures before you lock any slots.
🏰
Versailles is a full day out (~40 min by RER C, closed Mondays). If palace slots are afternoon-only, do the gardens and a canal lunch first, palace after.

🍸 Cocktail bars & speakeasies

Speakeasies are hidden bars with a concealed entrance (behind a freezer door, a launderette, no sign) — a Prohibition-era throwback Paris loves. You can absolutely go on your own — they're real bars: book a table and find the door. Small and popular — book a day or two ahead.
