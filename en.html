💳 Card-club / Viator discounts — worth it?

From your screenshots: Isracard's attractions are the Viator marketplace; finiti is a similar reseller. Their price is a reseller ticket (often "skip-the-line"), and the "−7%" / "from €…" is off their price, not the box office.

🏛️
Museums via a reseller — not worth it. Prices match or exceed the box office: Orsay €20–23 vs €16, Louvre €68 (a guided Mona-Lisa tour), Orangerie/Panthéon/Arc at or above gate. Your Museum Pass already covers all 8 paid sites (€105 vs ~€122 at the gate) with skip-the-line.
🗼
Eiffel Tower: the club's €59 (2nd floor + summit option) is more than the official ~€36–37 for the summit. Book on the official site.
⛵
Where a reseller genuinely helps: the Seine cruise ~€15 (vs ~€17–20 direct) and things not in your plan — Disneyland Paris €66 vs €82, Versailles with transfer, Atelier des Lumières, food & wine tours (~10–20% off).

🍸 Cocktail bars & speakeasies (hidden bars)

Speakeasies are hidden bars with a concealed entrance (behind a freezer door, a laundromat, no sign) — a Prohibition-era legacy, and Paris loves them. Yes, you can get in on your own — they're real bars: book a table and find the door. A "secret-bars tour" just adds a guide who knows the doors and "passwords", plus company — but it's not required. Bars are small and popular — book a day or two ahead.
